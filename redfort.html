About Red Fort

The Red Fort, located in Delhi, India, is a historic fort and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century.

The fort is known for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone and serves as a symbol of India's rich history. It was the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years.

Every year on Independence Day, the Prime Minister of India hoists the national flag at the main gate of the Red Fort and delivers a nationally broadcast speech from its ramparts.

Architecture

The Red Fort showcases the peak of Mughal architecture with its combination of Persian, Timurid, and Indian architectural styles. The fort complex includes many impressive structures such as the Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, and the Moti Masjid, each showcasing intricate designs and decorations.

History

Construction of the Red Fort began in 1638 and was completed in 1648. It served as the political and ceremonial center of the Mughal state, hosting important events and housing the imperial family. The fort remained a significant symbol of Mughal power until the British colonial period, when it was used as a military camp.
