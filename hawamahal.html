About Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, located in Jaipur, India, is a palace known for its unique and intricate architecture. It was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh.

The palace is renowned for its 953 small windows called jharokhas, decorated with intricate latticework. It was designed to allow royal ladies to observe street festivals while remaining unseen from the outside.

Hawa Mahal, also known as the Palace of Winds, is a symbol of the rich architectural heritage of Rajasthan.

Architecture

Hawa Mahal's architecture is a blend of Hindu Rajput and Islamic Mughal styles. The five-story exterior is akin to a honeycomb with small windows, allowing cool air to pass through, making the entire area more pleasant during the high temperatures in Jaipur.

History

Built in 1799, Hawa Mahal was constructed by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. Its purpose was to allow the royal ladies to observe the everyday life and festivals celebrated in the streets below without being seen, in line with the strict purdah system.
