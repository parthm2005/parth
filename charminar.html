About Charminar

Charminar, located in Hyderabad, India, is a historic monument and mosque built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah. It is a prominent landmark and symbol of Hyderabad.

The structure is made of granite, limestone, mortar, and pulverized marble. It has four grand arches facing four directions, with each side measuring 20 meters. The minarets are 56 meters high.

Charminar is situated at the heart of Hyderabad and is a major tourist attraction, drawing visitors from around the world.

Architecture

The architecture of Charminar is a fine example of Indo-Islamic architecture with Persian influences. The monument features intricate carvings, stucco decorations, and four minarets.

History

Charminar was built by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah to commemorate the end of a deadly plague. It is believed that he prayed for the end of the plague and vowed to build a mosque if his prayers were answered. The construction of Charminar marked the foundation of Hyderabad.
